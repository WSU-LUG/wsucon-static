Smash 4

February 5th @ 6pm

The Tournament Rules

General Rules

  1. You must be registered for the tournament.
  2. Double Elimination tournament
    1. All tournaments sets are best-of-3 except for Winner's Finals, Loser's Finals, and Grand Finals, which are best-of-5 sets.
  3. 2 Stocks, 6 minute time limit
    1. If time runs out, the player with more stocks wins.
    2. If both players have the same stock count, the player with the lower percentage count wins.
    3. If both players have the same stock and percentage count, start a new game with one stock for each player on the same stage with same characters.
  4. Items off
  5. Custom characters not allowed
  6. Only Miis with 1-1-1-1 moveset allowed
  7. All controllers (except those with performance-enhancing feature) allowed, including the GamePad
  8. Players not present 10 minutes after being called for their set will forfeit that set.

Stage Rules

  1. Starter Stages:
    1. Battlefield
    2. Final Destination
    3. Smashville
    4. Dreamland 64
    5. Town and City
  2. Counter-picks:
    1. Delfino Plaza
    2. Lylat Cruise
    3. Halberd
    4. Duck Hunt
    5. Castle Siege
    6. Kongo Jungle 64
  3. Counter-pick Alternates (legal counter-picks that are banned when their counterparts are):
    1. Any Omega Stage (alternate for Final Destination)
    2. Miiverse (alternate for Battlefield)

Stage Striking:

First game in set:

  1. Select characters:
    1. Either player can call for a "double-blind pick" during this section.
    2. Either player can also choose Random as a character.
  2. Play rock-paper-scissors with your opponent.
  3. The winner of the rock-paper-scissors game chooses to go first or second in the next step's stage striking process.
  4. Players then alternate "striking" a certain number of stages from the list of available starter stages until only one remains; the first game then takes place on that stage. The number of stages struck during each turn is as follows: 1-2-1.
  5. The first game is played; one player wins the first game.

All other games in set:

  1. In a best-of-3 set, the player who won the last game bans three stages from the full list of stages, which now includes the starters and counterpicks.
    1. In a best-of-5 set, only two stages are banned.
  2. The player who lost the last game then selects the next game's stage from any of the remaining stages that they have not won on previously in the set.
    1. a. If a player chooses a stage with a Counterpick Alternate here, they cannot change to a different alternate after this step.
  3. The player who won the last game then has the opportunity to switch characters.
  4. The player who lost the last game then has the opportunity to switch characters.
  5. The next game is played using the stages and characters chosen.
  6. Repeat steps 6 through 10 until a player wins the set.
